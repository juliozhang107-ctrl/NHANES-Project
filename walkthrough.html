USFLI vs. Gold Standard Transient Elastography

A comparative epidemiological analysis of MASLD, MASH, and Advanced Liver Fibrosis / Cirrhosis cohorts using data from the National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES).

PART A: Primary Study (NHANES 1999–2023)

The primary study evaluates non-invasive blood-based indexes—the U.S. Fatty Liver Index (USFLI > 30) for steatosis [1], FIB-4 > 2.67 for high-risk MASH [2], and APRI thresholds for fibrosis/cirrhosis [3].

PART B: Gold Standard Study (NHANES 2017–2023)

The gold standard study restricted the sample to the 5,699 adults aged ≥ 20 years in the morning fasting subsample who had valid transient elastography (FibroScan) measurements in the 2017–2020 pre-pandemic and 2021–2023 cycles.

Survey weights were combined to represent the 6-year period:

  • 2017–2020 Fasting weights were scaled by 2/3.
  • 2021–2023 Fasting weights were scaled by 1/3.

B.1. Gold Standard Cohort Sizes

  • MASLD (CAP ≥ 285 dB/m + CMR) [4,5]: Raw N = 2,586
  • MASH (MASLD + FAST ≥ 0.35) [6]: Raw N = 417
  • Cirrhosis (MASLD + LSM > 13.6 kPa) [7]: Raw N = 172

Mortality Data Availability Note: No public-use Linked Mortality Files are available for NHANES cycles after 2018 (including the 2019-2020 pre-pandemic and 2021-2023 cohorts). In addition, because NCHS re-assigned participant SEQN IDs in the combined 2017-2020 pre-pandemic cycle (P_DEMO), linkage of the 2017-2018 subset is not possible using public files. Thus, mortality statistics are marked as N/A.
